Born in Glasgow.
Built on Passion.

We opened our doors in 2011, in the heart of Merchant City, with one simple conviction: that Glasgow deserved a table worth sitting at. Not in spite of what this city is, but because of it.

Our head chef, Alasdair Murray, trained across Europe, spending years in kitchens in Lyon and Copenhagen. But home kept calling. He came back to Glasgow not to import something foreign, but to cook for his own city, on his own terms, with the extraordinary produce Scotland has always had.

Every week we source from Scottish farms and waters: Loch Fyne for shellfish, Perthshire for lamb, Highland estates for venison. Ayrshire dairy, seasonal foraged greens, day-boat fish landed on the west coast. We cook what Scotland grows, with the craft it deserves.

Glasgow is not a city of pretension. It is warm, direct, and fiercely proud. That is how we run our restaurant.
